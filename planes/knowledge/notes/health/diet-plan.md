# Acne Diet Guidelines

This document summarizes dietary factors that may influence acne based on dermatology research and personal observations.

---

# Key Dietary Factors

## High Glycemic Foods (Strong Evidence)

Foods that rapidly increase blood sugar can worsen acne by increasing insulin and IGF-1, which stimulate oil production.

Common high-glycemic foods:

- candy
- desserts
- sugary drinks
- sweet tea / soda
- white bread
- white rice
- pastries
- sweet snacks

Effects:

- increased oil production
- more clogged pores
- more whiteheads
- increased inflammatory acne

Guideline:

Reduce frequent consumption of high-sugar and high-refined carbohydrate foods.

Prefer:

- whole grains
- vegetables
- lower glycemic fruits

---

## Dairy (Moderate Evidence)

Some studies suggest dairy may worsen acne in certain individuals.

Potential triggers:

- milk
- skim milk
- whey protein supplements

Possible mechanism:

- hormonal signaling
- increased IGF-1

Not everyone is sensitive to dairy.

Guideline:

If acne persists, consider reducing dairy intake for several weeks and monitor changes.

---

## Oily or Greasy Foods (Weak Evidence)

Eating oily foods does **not directly increase skin oil production**.

Examples:

- fried food
- fatty meats
- oily noodles

These foods may correlate with acne when they contain:

- refined carbohydrates
- sugary sauces
- high calorie intake

Guideline:

Moderate intake but no strict restriction required.

# Beneficial Foods

Some nutrients may support skin health and reduce inflammation.

Recommended foods:

### Omega-3 Fatty Acids
- salmon
- sardines
- mackerel
- flaxseed
- walnuts

Benefits:

- anti-inflammatory
- may reduce acne severity

---

### Zinc-Rich Foods

- pumpkin seeds
- beef
- chickpeas
- lentils
- nuts

Benefits:

- supports immune function
- may reduce acne inflammation

---

### Vegetables and Fiber

- leafy greens
- broccoli
- carrots
- cucumbers

Benefits:

- stabilizes blood sugar
- supports gut health

---

# Lifestyle Factors Affecting Acne

Diet interacts with other important factors:

### Sleep
Poor sleep can increase stress hormones and worsen acne.

### Stress
Stress increases cortisol which can stimulate oil production.

### Hydration
Adequate water intake supports skin barrier function.

---

# Personal Observations

Based on skin patterns:

Nose:
- blackheads mainly related to oil production

Forehead:
- likely influenced by stress and sleep quality

Around mouth:
- whiteheads possibly linked to sugar intake

---

# Practical Guidelines

Focus on:

- reducing sugar intake
- maintaining balanced meals
- prioritizing vegetables and whole foods
- managing stress and sleep

Moderation rather than strict elimination is recommended.

---

# Monitoring Strategy

Track:

- sugar intake
- dairy consumption
- stress levels
- sleep quality
- acne flare-ups

Look for patterns over several weeks.
