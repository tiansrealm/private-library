# Why Pleasing Women Doesn't Work

**Source:** Jun Ge (君哥)
**Date Added:** 2026-03-11

---

## It's Not Your Fault

- Many men instinctively try to please women, even when they know they shouldn't
- This behavior isn't a lack of willpower — it comes from deeply ingrained thinking patterns learned over time

## Society Shaped This Mindset

- From a young age, men are taught: "Treat her well, and she will like you"
- Parents, peers, and media all reinforce this idea
- Experience proves otherwise — being overly generous or attentive doesn't guarantee affection

## The Historical Context

- In the past, women relied on men for survival — simple gestures or material offerings could secure love
- Men dominated socially; relationships operated under very different conditions
- Divorce rates were low; women's independence was limited

## Modern Reality Is Different

- Today, women are independent and often financially or socially better off than men
- The old strategies of "buying affection" or over-pleasing no longer work
- Attraction is built on mutual respect, value, and emotional intelligence — not just "being good"

---

## Core Message

> Your instinct to please women isn't inherently wrong, but it's based on outdated social norms. Modern relationships require a shift: value yourself, maintain dignity, and build attraction through genuine confidence and mutual investment rather than overgiving.
