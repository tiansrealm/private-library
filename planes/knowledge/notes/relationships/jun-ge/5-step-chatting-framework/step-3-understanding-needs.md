# Step 3: Understanding Her Needs

**Source:** Jun Ge (君哥) — Five-Step Chatting Method
**Date Added:** 2026-03-11

---

## Concept

**Goal:** Identify her needs, concerns, and interests by paying attention to what she shares.

Knowing her needs lays the groundwork for deeper connection and emotional closeness.

---

## What to Observe

- Work or study situation
- Family background
- Hobbies and interests
- Life stage and experience level

---

## How to Discover Needs

1. **Observe and listen** — don't interrogate; let her share naturally
2. **Exchange information naturally** — share your own experiences, work, school life
3. **Match topic complexity to her experience level**
   - A college junior → don't talk about office politics (becomes one-sided)
   - Meet her where she is
4. **Subtle scenario-building (画面感)**
   - Plant mental images or mini-scenarios that evoke feelings and connection
   - Example: "I'll guide you through this like your teacher showing you something new" → playful, innocent imagery of guidance and closeness

---

## Why This Works

- Girls often can't respond if the topic is too complex or outside their experience
- Matching the topic to her level makes the conversation engaging and easy to participate in
- When you later do similar things in real life, they feel natural and emotionally consistent

---

## Practical Examples

| Scenario | Example | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| College junior worried about internships | "I've been through this too; here's what you can expect..." | Provides guidance, builds trust |
| Teacher with specific routine | Learn about her work, hobbies, lifestyle | Sets context for future connection |
| Subtle scene-building | "I'll guide you through this as your teacher..." | Evokes playful mental imagery, prepares for real-life interaction |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Pay attention to what she reveals — work, hobbies, life stage
2. Discover needs through natural conversation, not interrogation
3. Match topic complexity to her experience to avoid awkwardness
4. Use subtle scenario-building to plant feelings and emotional context
5. This foundation makes later flirtation and relationship development natural
