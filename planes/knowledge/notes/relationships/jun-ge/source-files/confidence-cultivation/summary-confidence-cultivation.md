## Core Thesis

Confidence is the #1 trait that makes a man attractive. Women instinctively feel it and relax around confident men, sensing "this man can meet my needs and protect me." Lack of confidence triggers the opposite — she raises her guard.

## Key Points

1. **Confidence radiates inside-out** — believe in yourself first, then it shows through eyes, speech, and body language.

2. **The confident attitude:** "No problem. I can definitely solve this." Even if you fail now, you believe you'll handle it eventually.

3. **Confidence comes from experience** — everything is uncertain the first time. By the 3rd, 4th, 5th time, you handle it smoothly. Just like cutting vegetables: terrifying at first, automatic after years.

4. **Reframe failure as progress:**
   - Rejected today? → "At least I wasn't nervous this time — one step closer."
   - Bad date? → "I accumulated more dating experience — next time will be smoother."

5. **You are the center of your own world** — revolve around yourself, not around a specific person or event. Focus on your growth trajectory, not individual outcomes.

6. **The author's example:** First speech on stage — mind went blank, voice trembled, kept sweating. Now speaks confidently to 100+ people. The key was focusing on self-improvement rather than the event's outcome.

## Actionable Takeaway

Stop waiting to "feel confident" — go do the thing, accumulate experience, reframe setbacks as data points, and confidence builds naturally through repetition.
